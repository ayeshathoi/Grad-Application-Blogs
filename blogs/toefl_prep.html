Description

TOEFL is a standardized test that evaluates the English proficiency of non-native English speakers, necessary for admission to universities in English-speaking countries. Scoring minimum 90 is required for most grad universities. Some wants may be higher or lower. You can select 4 universities here until 10 pm, the day before your test day.

According to new TOEFL iBT test, 2023

Section Duration Pattern Questions Score
Reading 35 minutes 2 Academic passages 10 questions each * 2 = 20 30
Listening 36 minutes 3 Lectures (classroom discussions) & 2 Conversations 28 Question 30
Speaking 16 minutes 4 tasks 1 independent and 3 integrated 30
Writing 30 minutes 2 tasks 1 integrated and 1 academic 30

Requirements

1. Passport
2. ETS Account Creation and TOEFL Registration
3. TOEFL Test Fee $205 - payment has to be done by Credit Card
4. Take printed copy of registration and Passport on the test day

For starters, you should register for the TOEFL exam before preparing. 14 days with two hours each day are enough for the exam. It will boost your seriousness and commitment to the exam.

Guidelines

First of all, take a monthly subscription of Testglider after registering your exam. It helps a lot. If you take monthly subscription before registering, you won't have access of the question after 1 month.

1. Reading
- Don't read whole passage when it starts
- Question difficulty is higher than testglider/ets
- Reading has summarization questions in each passage. To answer them, write/understand the overall idea of each paragraph.
- In test day, time goes faster, so practice as if your time is 28 minutes.
- Don't spend too much time on a single question.

2. Listening
- In the real test, talks are in much higher speed than ets/testglider.
- You can listen youtube video with 1.5x speed
- Take notes while listening.

3. Speaking
- Record your voice and listen to it.
- "My_Speaking_Score" website uses speechrater which is used to grade in real test. In that case, you need to buy subscription.
- Watch a video on which factors are included for grading the speaking [Speed, pause, fluency etc.].
- Make a template.
- My Speaking Template

4. Writing
- Write about 300 words for the task 1 and 135 words for second one
- Make a template
- Check your grammar mistake in grammarly. Otherwise, silly mistakes can hamper your score.
- My Writing Template
